## Collections Management

### How do I create a new collection?

1. Click the collection dropdown in the top navigation
2. Select "Create New Collection"
3. Enter a name for your collection
4. Choose the collection type (watches, sneakers, or purses)
5. Click "Create"

### Can I change a collection's type after creating it?

No. Collection type is permanent once set. This is because each type has different fields and data structures. If you need to change the type, you'll need to create a new collection and manually transfer items.

### How do I delete a collection?

1. Select the collection you want to delete
2. Go to Settings â†’ Collection Settings
3. Scroll to "Danger Zone"
4. Click "Delete Collection"
5. Confirm by typing the collection name

**Warning**: This action cannot be undone. All items, wear logs, and data will be permanently deleted.

### Can I share my collection with others?

Yes! You can share collections with three permission levels:

- **Viewer**: Can only view items (read-only)
- **Editor**: Can add, edit, and delete items
- **Owner**: Full control, can delete collection and manage users

To share:
1. Open the collection you want to share
2. Click Settings â†’ Sharing
3. Enter the user's email
4. Choose permission level
5. Send invite

### Can someone be an owner of my collection?

Yes. You can have multiple owners for a collection. All owners have equal permissions. This is useful for:
- Couples managing a shared collection
- Business partners tracking inventory
- Family collections

### How do I accept a collection invitation?

When someone shares a collection with you:
1. You'll receive an email notification
2. Click the link in the email
3. Log in to your account
4. Click "Accept" to add the collection to your account

The collection will appear in your collection dropdown.

## Collection Types: Watches

### What watch brands are supported?

All watch brands are supported! Common ones include:
- Luxury: Rolex, Patek Philippe, Audemars Piguet, Omega
- Premium: Tudor, Grand Seiko, IWC, Jaeger-LeCoultre
- Affordable: Seiko, Citizen, Orient, Timex
- Smartwatches: Apple Watch, Garmin, Samsung Galaxy Watch

### What is "movement type"?

Movement type describes how the watch operates:
- **Automatic**: Self-winding mechanical (winds while wearing)
- **Manual**: Hand-wound mechanical
- **Quartz**: Battery-powered electronic
- **Smartwatch**: Digital/smart features

### Should I include smartwatches?

Yes! Smartwatches are fully supported. While some fields (like "movement") might not apply, you can track purchase date, cost, model, and usage just like traditional watches.

### What is "water resistance testing"?

Water resistance testing logs track when you expose your watch to water:
- Swimming
- Diving
- Rain exposure
- Washing hands

This helps you monitor if the watch maintains its water resistance rating over time.

### Do I need to log every watch wearing?

It's up to you! Logging wear helps with:
- Cost per wear calculations
- Understanding which watches get used most
- Rotation planning
- Resale justification

Many users log daily, while others log monthly or for special occasions only.

## Collection Types: Sneakers

### What does "colorway" mean?

Colorway is the color scheme/design of the sneaker. Examples:
- "Chicago" (Air Jordan 1 - white, red, black)
- "Bred" (Black/Red)
- "Triple White"
- "Panda" (Dunk - white with black)

### What's the difference between SKU and Style Code?

- **SKU** (Stock Keeping Unit): Retailer's inventory code
- **Style Code**: Manufacturer's product code (Nike, Adidas, etc.)

Example for Nike Air Jordan 1 Chicago:
- Style Code: 555088-101
- SKU: Varies by retailer

Both are useful for authentication and resale tracking.

### What does "deadstock" mean?

Sneaker condition terminology:
- **Deadstock (DS)**: Brand new, never worn, original everything
- **VNDS** (Very Near Deadstock): Tried on or worn once indoors
- **Used**: Worn multiple times, shows wear

### What is "OG all"?

"OG all" means Original All - the sneaker comes with all original accessories:
- Original box
- Extra laces
- Hang tags
- Inserts/tissue paper
- Receipt
- Authenticity cards

This is important for collectible/limited edition sneakers.

### Should I include size for all regions?

Pick the region you normally use (US, UK, or EU). The platform doesn't auto-convert because sizing varies by brand. You only need one.

Always photograph serial numbers and record them. Keep them private in your collection settings if you're concerned about privacy.

## Tracking & Statistics

### What is "cost per wear"?

Cost per wear = Purchase cost Ã· Total days worn

This metric helps you understand the value of an item. Examples:
- $5,000 watch worn 500 days = $10/wear
- $200 sneakers worn 20 days = $10/wear

Lower cost per wear = better value from your purchase.

### How are "most worn" items calculated?

Based on total days logged in wear entries. If you wear a watch for 2 days on a trip, that counts as 2 days toward the total.

### Can I track items I don't own yet?

Yes! Use the **Wishlist** feature:
1. Go to Wishlist tab
2. Add items you want to acquire
3. Track brand, model, target price
4. Get notified of trade matches with friends

### Do trips and events work differently per collection type?

They work the same way across all types:
1. Create a trip or event
2. Link items you brought/wore
3. Specify days per item
4. Add notes and photos

This helps track which items go with you on adventures!

## Privacy & Security

### Who can see my collection?

By default, collections are private (only you). You can change this in Settings:
- **Private**: Only you and users you've explicitly shared with
- **Friends Only**: Your friends can view
- **Public**: Anyone with the link can view (read-only)

### Can I hide purchase prices?

Yes! In collection privacy settings:
- Toggle "Hide purchase costs in public view"
- Costs will only be visible to you
- Other viewers see items without prices

### Is my data backed up?

Yes! Your data is:
- Backed up daily on Supabase infrastructure
- Stored with redundancy across multiple servers
- Encrypted at rest and in transit

**Recommendation**: Export your data monthly as an additional backup.

### How do I enable two-factor authentication (2FA)?

1. Go to Settings â†’ Security
2. Click "Enable 2FA"
3. Scan QR code with authenticator app (Google Authenticator, Authy, 1Password)
4. Enter verification code
5. Save recovery codes in a safe place

**Highly recommended** if your collection has significant value!

## Troubleshooting

### My photos aren't uploading

**Check:**
- File size under 10MB
- Supported format (JPG, PNG, HEIC)
- Stable internet connection
- Browser permissions for file upload

**Try:**
- Compress large photos
- Clear browser cache
- Try a different browser
- Use mobile app instead

### I can't see my friend's collection

**Check:**
- Friend has shared the collection with you
- You've accepted the invitation
- Collection privacy settings allow your permission level
- You're logged into the correct account

### My stats seem wrong

**Check:**
- Wear logs are properly saved
- Items are in "active" status (not sold/traded)
- Correct collection is selected
- Dates are valid

**Try:**
- Refresh the page
- Clear browser cache
- Re-sync data (Settings â†’ Advanced â†’ Re-sync)

### The app is slow

**Check:**
- Internet connection speed
- Large number of high-res photos (compress them)
- Browser cache (clear it)
- Device storage (free up space)

**Try:**
- Use list view instead of grid view
- Limit photo sizes
- Close other browser tabs
- Update to latest version
